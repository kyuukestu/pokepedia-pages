Trainer 101
The Three Pillars of Transit

To ensure the safety of both humans and Pokémon, the League oversees three specialized regulatory bodies. Each authority manages a specific domain of movement, providing certification, safety guidelines, and infrastructure support for trainers worldwide.

Aviation Permit (AP)

Governed by the AAA. Required for aerial transit, spanning from personal flight to public Corviknight-Taxis.

Explore

Maritime Permit (MP)

Governed by the MMM. Covers surface surfing and deep-sea diving across international waters.

Explore

Terrestrial Permit (TP)

Governed by the TTT. Regulations for high-speed land mounts and heavy-load mountain traversal.

Universal Safety Standards

Regardless of the medium—air, sea, or land—certain universal laws apply to all operators. These "Global Statutes" are the foundation of safe exploration.

Trainer ID Linkage

All permits are digitally bound to your Trainer ID. Suspensions in one sector may affect your standing in others.

Mount Readiness

Regular health checks are mandatory to ensure mounts are physically capable of safe transport.

Cross-Regional Validity

Thanks to international treaties, permits issued by the AAA, MMM, and TTT are recognized in all major regions.

General Requirements

Trainer Eligibility

  • Minimum Age: 10 years old for Basic (Class C) certification.
  • Health Clearance: Biennial medical and psych-eval certificates.
  • Conduct: No history of Pokémon neglect or reckless operation.

The Licensing Process

While specific hours vary, all licensing follows a standard 5-step pipeline:

1. Application
2. Theory School
3. Practical Hours
4. Examination
5. Registry
Operator Advisory
Attempting to use moves like Fly, Surf, or high-speed land travel in urban centers without the appropriate license and IFF tag is a federal offense. Local authorities are equipped with specialized interceptor teams to ground or dock unauthorized travelers.
GLOBAL RECIPROCITY: Licenses revoked in one region are automatically invalidated worldwide. Always ensure your mount and permit are up to date before attempting trans-regional crossings.
